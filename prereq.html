About The Chemistry Challenge Exam

The Chemistry Challenge Exam is held four times a semester. The exam is only for students who are challenging the prerequisite to enroll in Chemistry 01A,    which is successful completion of Chemistry 015.   Below is an outline of the procedure for reserving a spot to attemp the Chemistry Challenge Exam:

Prior to scheduling an appointment for the exam, students are to obtain a Prerequisite Challenge Form.  The Prerequisite Challenge Form is to be formally initiated by an academic counselor.  Students should contact the Counseling Department at (408) 288-3750 to schedule a counseling appointment or to learn about counseling drop-in dates/times. After a student obtains a signed Prerequisite Challenge Form, students must contact the & Science Division Office at (408) 288-3716 or visit room S-201 (in the Science Complex) to reserve a seat for the exam.

Students will be notified of the exam results, via email, by the end of day after completion of the Chemistry Challenge Exam. To be recommended to bypass Chemistry 015 and enroll in Chemistry 01A students must provide a correct response to 17 out of 24 exam questions.  That is, earn a score of 70% or higher on the exam.

Students are encouraged to work with an academic counselor so as to develop a plan to complete the Chemistry Challenge Exam during the regular Spring and Fall semester.  Currently the Chemistry Department does not have a process to offer the Prerequisite Challenge Exam during the campus closure period.

A student may attempt the Chemistry Challenge Exam one time per semester. The Chemistry Prerequisite Challenge Exam is a 60 minute exam, consisting of 24 multiple choice type questions. As such, students will have 60 minutes to complete the exam. To be recommended for enrollment in Chemistry 01A the student must provide the correct answer, to a minimum, of 17 out of the 24 exam questions. This amounts to earning a minimum score of 70% on the exam.

Exam questions pertain to course content covered in Chemistry 015.   The list below contains Chemistry 015 topics organized by chapter.  Students are encouraged to use the list of topics covered in Chemistry 015 as well as the Chemistry 015 student learning outcomes as a guide to prepare for the exam. Chemistry 015 Student Learning Outcomes

  1. Apply safety rules learned in the lab to safely conduct lab operations and present laboratory data using graphing and precision of data wih simple statistics.

  2. Distinguish between elements and compounds, their physical and chemical properties, along with ionic and covalent compounds, and the writting of their names and formulas.

  3. Describe the internal structure of atoms using subatomic particles, the meaning nuclear symbols for isotopes while predicting the nature of chemical bonds and and molecular polarity from the periodicity of the element.

  4. Explain the concepts of chemical stoichiometry at both the macroscopic and particulate level while applying these concepts to solving chemical reaction problems from rea-world information and predicting products of simple chemical reaction types.

  5. Explain gas behavior using macroscopic properties and microscopic molecular dynsmics and solve problems of gases using the combined gas law and ideal gas law.

  6. Explain how liquids, solids, and solution behavior can be understood in terms of the intermolecular forces involved and kinetic molecular theory. Solve solution concentration problems along with explaining acid-base reactions and solution conductive behavior.
